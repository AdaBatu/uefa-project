\block{Research question: why does Napoli's possession not become enough shot value?}
{
\Large
Napoli can keep the ball and enter advanced zones, but possession often loses value at the
final connection: longer passes are less secure, final-third actions are harder to complete,
and many shots arrive from crowded or medium-quality positions.

\medskip
\begin{tabular}{>{\bfseries}p{0.18\linewidth}p{0.78\linewidth}}
Report finding &
Strong circulation and progression, especially through central midfield and the left half-space,
but too few high-value final actions.\\
Poster focus &
We connect \textbf{pass length and completion}, \textbf{attacking benchmark percentiles},
\textbf{shot location and xG}, and \textbf{ball-loss danger}.\\
Main message &
Keep attacking ambition, but add cleaner support and better final-third timing so entries
become cut-backs, lay-offs, and clearer shots.
\end{tabular}
}

\medskip
\normalsize
The shot map reinforces the report's main attacking conclusion. Napoli frequently reach the

lane while defenders can still contest the shot.

\medskip
The larger red circles show that Napoli can create and finish good chances, but those moments
are not frequent enough. Many smaller shots come after achieving territory, not after fully
breaking the defensive structure.
}

The heatmap adds the defensive cost of the attacking problem. When Napoli lose the ball in

shots at an above-average rate. Napoli can keep attacking through these zones, but need better
cover behind the ball before the risky pass or shot.
}

\block{Recommendations}
{
\normalsize
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Keep the possession base.} Short and medium passing gives control and field position.
\item \textbf{Improve the pass before the shot.} Use cut-backs, wall passes, and third-player runs.
\item \textbf{Use long passes with support.} Direct balls need nearby second-action coverage.
\item \textbf{Protect the loss zones.} Central and left-sided turnovers need closer midfield cover.
\end{enumerate}
}

\block{Conclusion}
{
\large
Napoli's issue is not access; it is converting access into chance quality. The passing and radar
plots show stable progression, the shot map shows attacks ending before the defence is fully opened,
and the loss-to-shot heatmap shows the transition cost. The fix is cleaner final actions with an elite striker. 
}
